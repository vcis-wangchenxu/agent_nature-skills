# Nature-style readiness rubric for RL/MARL/LLM/Agent manuscripts

Score each axis from 0 to 5.

1. **Big-question fit**: Does the paper address a broad scientific or technological question beyond one benchmark score?
2. **Contribution clarity**: Is the core mechanism new, necessary and distinguishable from prior work?
3. **Evidence strength**: Are results shown across appropriate tasks, seeds, baselines, ablations and stress tests?
4. **Generalization**: Are claims tested on held-out tasks, partners, models, environments or distribution shifts?
5. **Mechanistic insight**: Does the paper explain why the method works or fails, not only that it improves numbers?
6. **Reproducibility**: Can a competent researcher reproduce training, evaluation, prompts, tools and figures?
7. **Writing accessibility**: Can a broad AI/science audience understand the problem, method and significance?
8. **Risk control**: Are safety, misuse, benchmark contamination, compute cost and limitations handled honestly?

Readiness interpretation:

- 0–15: internal draft only.
- 16–25: workshop or early conference draft.
- 26–32: solid conference submission after revision.
- 33–40: plausible high-impact submission if novelty is strong.
